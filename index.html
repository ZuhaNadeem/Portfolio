Final Year Project - Multi-Factor Crop Yield Optimization Toolkit

In this project, a novel approach to crop yield optimization is presented, focusing on the development of a toolkit capable of finding global optima without the need for mathematical or functional representations of the underlying data or problem. Traditional optimization methods often require explicit mathematical models, which can be challenging to derive for complex agricultural systems. The proposed toolkit employs multi-factor Bayesian optimization, different machine learning models to achieve its goal. The toolkit is designed to handle agricultural systems' inherent uncertainty and nonlinearity, offering a versatile and efficient solution for farmers and agronomists. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the toolkit in optimizing crop yields, showcasing its potential to revolutionize agricultural practices. This project contributes to the field of agricultural optimization by providing a robust and adaptable toolkit that can significantly enhance crop yield predictions and management strategies, ultimately leading to improved agricultural sustainability and food security.

Predictive Maintenance Model Development using CNN and GNN

This project aimed to develop a predictive maintenance model using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Graph Neural Networks (GNN).

Problem Statement: The project addressed the challenge of predicting equipment failures in advance to enable proactive maintenance, thus reducing downtime and maintenance costs.

Approach and Tools: The team employed a combination of CNN and GNN to analyze sensor data and equipment graphs. Python was used for coding, and libraries such as TensorFlow and PyTorch were utilized for deep learning implementations.

Challenges and Solutions: One major challenge was effectively integrating the CNN and GNN outputs. This was overcome by developing a hybrid model that combined the strengths of both architectures, enhancing predictive accuracy.

Results: The model achieved an accuracy of 93% in predicting equipment failures, surpassing the initial target. The outcomes were visualized through data plots and performance metrics, demonstrating the effectiveness of the developed solution.

Object Detection and Distance Measurement for a Car using TM4C123GH6PM and Ultrasonic Sensor

This project focuses on implementing object detection and distance measurement capabilities for a car utilizing the TM4C123GH6PM microcontroller and an ultrasonic sensor. The system enables the vehicle to detect obstacles and accurately measure their distance for safe navigation.

Problem Statement: The project addresses the need for vehicles to have advanced sensing capabilities to ensure safe driving and obstacle avoidance.

Approach and Tools: The team utilized the TM4C123GH6PM microcontroller and an ultrasonic sensor to develop the object detection and distance measurement system. Embedded C programming was used for coding, and the system was tested and integrated into a car prototype.

Challenges and Solutions: Challenges included sensor accuracy and real-time processing. Solutions involved calibrating the sensor and optimizing the software for faster processing.

Results: The system successfully detected objects and measured distances with high accuracy, enhancing the safety features of the car.
